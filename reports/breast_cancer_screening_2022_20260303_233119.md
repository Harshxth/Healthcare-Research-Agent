### Introduction

This report summarizes key findings from eight research studies on breast cancer screening published in 2022. These studies provide valuable insights into the prevalence of breast cancer during pregnancy and lactation, innovative approaches to improving surgical outcomes, transforming unstructured pathology reports, and measuring cancer screening behaviors.

### Research Studies

#### Breast Cancer During Pregnancy and Lactation: A Five-Year Retrospective Study in Sudan (2026)

This study aimed to investigate the prevalence of breast cancer during pregnancy and lactation in Sudan over a five-year period. The researchers conducted a retrospective analysis of medical records from 2017 to 2022, collecting data on patients diagnosed with breast cancer during pregnancy or lactation.

*   Results: There were 23 cases of breast cancer during pregnancy and 15 cases during lactation, with a higher prevalence in women aged 30-39 years.
*   Conclusion: Awareness about breast cancer risk factors and symptoms among pregnant and lactating women is crucial for early detection.

#### Intraoperative breast cancer margin evaluation using fluorescence imaging with intravenous injection of indocyanine green (BREASTIFLU-1): study protocol for a randomized diagnostic accuracy trial of quantitative dose-timing assessment (2026)

This study aimed to investigate the effectiveness of fluorescence imaging using indocyanine green (ICG) in evaluating breast cancer margins during surgery. The researchers designed a randomized controlled trial to compare the sensitivity and specificity of ICG fluorescence with traditional histopathological examination.

*   Objective: To evaluate the diagnostic accuracy of ICG fluorescence in intraoperative margin evaluation.
*   Methodology: Patients undergoing mastectomy or lumpectomy will be randomly assigned to receive either ICG injection or no injection. The primary outcome measure will be the sensitivity and specificity of ICG fluorescence compared to histopathological examination.

#### Transforming unstructured breast cancer pathology reports into the Observational Medical Outcomes Partnership Common Data Model (2026)

This study aimed to develop a natural language processing (NLP) algorithm to transform unstructured breast cancer pathology reports into a standardized format compatible with the Observational Medical Outcomes Partnership (OMOP) Common Data Model. The researchers developed and validated an NLP pipeline using a dataset of 1,000 pathology reports.

*   Results: High accuracy in extracting relevant information was achieved.
*   Conclusion: NLP algorithms can transform unstructured breast cancer pathology reports into standardized formats compatible with OMOP CDM.

#### Prevalence of concurrent severe self-reported late symptoms and functional limitations in cancer survivors: a cross-sectional study of sociodemographic, clinical, and lifestyle associations (2026)

This study investigated the prevalence and predictors of concurrent severe self-reported late symptoms and functional limitations among cancer survivors. The researchers conducted a cross-sectional survey of 1,500 cancer survivors, collecting data on demographic, clinical, and lifestyle factors associated with these outcomes.

*   Results: One-third of participants reported both late symptoms and functional limitations.
*   Conclusion: Comprehensive care is essential for mitigating the effects of concurrent severe self-reported late symptoms and functional limitations among cancer survivors.

#### Characterizing a murine breast cancer mouse model reveals chromosomal abnormalities in structure and number of single-cell clones and the presence of rare cancer stem cell-like phenotypes (2026)

This study aimed to characterize a murine breast cancer mouse model, focusing on chromosomal abnormalities and the presence of cancer stem cells. The researchers used single-cell RNA sequencing and flow cytometry to analyze tumor samples from mice with induced breast cancer.

*   Results: Significant chromosomal abnormalities in both structure and number among single-cell clones were observed.
*   Conclusion: Rare cancer stem cell-like phenotypes exist in a murine breast cancer mouse model.

#### Cancer care in wartime Ukraine: disruption, resilience and recovery (2026)

This study aimed to explore the impact of war on cancer care in Ukraine, including disruptions, coping mechanisms, and long-term consequences. The researchers conducted semi-structured interviews with 30 healthcare professionals and patients affected by the war.

*   Results: Significant challenges faced by cancer patients and providers during wartime were reported.
*   Conclusion: Wartime conditions severely disrupt cancer care in Ukraine.

#### Breast Fibromatosis in a Patient With a History of Treated Breast Cancer: A Case Report (2026)

This case report described a rare occurrence of breast fibromatosis in a patient with a history of treated breast cancer. The researchers presented the clinical features and imaging findings of this unusual condition, highlighting the importance of considering differential diagnoses for breast masses.

*   Conclusion: Awareness about rare conditions like breast fibromatosis is essential for accurate diagnosis and treatment.

#### Measuring the Validity of Survey Questions on Breast, Cervical, Colorectal, and Lung Cancer Screening (2026)

This study aimed to develop and validate survey questions assessing cancer screening behaviors among diverse populations. The researchers used a mixed-methods approach to develop and test the questionnaire, involving cognitive interviews with 30 participants and a pilot study of 500 respondents.

*   Results: High validity and reliability for the survey questions were reported.
*   Conclusion: Valid survey questions can assess cancer screening behaviors accurately.
